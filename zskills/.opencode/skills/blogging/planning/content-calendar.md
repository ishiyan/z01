# Content Calendar

Plan and schedule a series of blog articles covering a subject. Includes content mix, cadence, decay detection, and distribution timing.

## When to Use

- Planning a blog series (3+ articles on related topics)
- Setting up a regular publishing cadence
- Deciding what to write next based on content gaps
- Scheduling content refreshes for existing posts

## Step 1: Define the Series

Before generating a calendar, clarify:

1. **Subject**: What broad topic does this series cover?
2. **Audience**: Who are you writing for? (skill level, role, goals)
3. **Cadence**: How often can you realistically publish? (weekly, biweekly, monthly)
4. **Total posts**: How many articles in the series? (5-12 is typical)
5. **Goal**: Traffic? Authority? Lead generation? Community building?

## Step 2: Topic Clustering

Organize articles into clusters with a hub-and-spoke model:

```
Pillar Article (comprehensive, 3000-4000 words)
├── Supporting Article 1 (specific sub-topic, 1500-2500 words)
├── Supporting Article 2
├── Supporting Article 3
└── Supporting Article 4
```

Each supporting article links to the pillar and vice versa. This builds topical authority for search.

Aim for 3-5 clusters per calendar quarter.

## Step 3: Content Mix Formula

Balance content types for sustainable publishing:

| Type | Ratio | Purpose |
|------|-------|---------|
| New content | 60% | Fresh articles covering new ground |
| Refreshes | 30% | Update existing posts (stats, examples, links) |
| Repurposed | 10% | Transform existing content for new formats |

## Step 4: Sequencing

Order articles for maximum impact:

1. **Pillar first**: Establish the comprehensive overview
2. **High-translatability supporting articles next**: These generate social media distribution
3. **Deep-dive / technical articles later**: Build on established foundation
4. **FAQ / roundup articles last**: Fill gaps and answer remaining questions

## Step 5: Freshness Schedule

| Content Age | Action | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| 30 days | Check if statistics are still current | High-traffic posts |
| 90 days | Review for accuracy, add new developments | All posts |
| 6 months | Major refresh (new examples, updated data) | Pillar pages |
| 12 months | Full rewrite if the field has shifted | All posts |

## Step 6: Distribution Timing

For each published article, schedule distribution:

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| Day 0 (publish) | Substack Note + LinkedIn post |
| Day 1 | X thread |
| Day 2-3 | Medium cross-post (set canonical to original) |
| Day 7 | Cross-post blurb to partner newsletters |
| Day 14 | Reddit thread in relevant subreddit (if post has genuine value to the community) |

## Calendar Template

```markdown
## [Month] Content Calendar

### Week 1: [Date]
- **Publish**: [Title] (Template: [type], Words: [target])
  - Cluster: [pillar/supporting]
  - Distribution: LinkedIn Day 0, X Day 1, Medium Day 3
  
### Week 2: [Date]
- **Refresh**: [Existing post title]
  - Update: [what needs updating]
  - New dateModified in schema

### Week 3: [Date]
- **Publish**: [Title] (Template: [type], Words: [target])
  - Cluster: [pillar/supporting]
  - Distribution: LinkedIn Day 0, X Day 1

### Week 4: [Date]
- **Repurpose**: [Source post] → [New format]
  - E.g., "Turn 3 posts into a comparison table article"
```

## Content Pillar Ratio

For general-audience blogs, balance topics:

| Pillar Type | Ratio | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Educational | 40% | Teach skills, explain concepts |
| Inspirational | 25% | Case studies, success stories, personal essays |
| Conversational | 25% | Opinion, hot takes, community discussion |
| Promotional | 10% | Product features, announcements (only if you have a product) |

## Decay Detection

Signs a post needs refreshing:

- Traffic declining month-over-month for 3+ months
- Statistics older than 1 year
- Links returning 404
- Comments asking questions the post should answer
- Competitors outranking with newer content
- Field has materially changed since publication

## Guardrails

1. Never plan more content than you can realistically produce at quality. Publishing late is worse than publishing less frequently.
2. Every calendar item must have a specific topic (not "write something about X").
3. Refreshes count as publishing. A well-refreshed post with updated data often outperforms new content.
4. Don't schedule distribution for posts that failed the quality rubric. Fix first.
