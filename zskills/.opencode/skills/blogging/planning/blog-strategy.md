# Blog Strategy

Positioning, topic clustering, audience mapping, differentiation, and measurement framework for a blog or content program.

## When to Use

- Starting a new blog from scratch
- Repositioning an existing blog that lacks direction
- Planning content for a new niche or audience
- Building topical authority in a specific domain

## Step 1: Discovery

Clarify these before creating strategy:

1. **What do you do / build / sell?** (or: what expertise do you have?)
2. **Who are you writing for?** (2-3 audience segments max)
3. **What are you trying to achieve?** (Traffic, authority, leads, community, hiring)
4. **Who else writes about this?** (3-5 competitors or adjacent blogs)
5. **What's your unfair advantage?** (Unique data, unique experience, unique access, unique perspective)
6. **How much can you publish?** (Realistic cadence given your constraints)

## Step 2: Audience Mapping

Define 2-3 audience segments:

```markdown
### Segment 1: [Name]
- **Role**: [Job title / context]
- **Skill level**: [Beginner / Intermediate / Advanced]
- **Pain points**: [What problems do they search for?]
- **Content preference**: [Depth vs breadth, format preference]
- **Where they hang out**: [Subreddits, Slack groups, conferences]
```

## Step 3: Topic Cluster Design

Build 3-5 topic clusters using hub-and-spoke:

```markdown
### Cluster 1: [Topic Area]
- **Pillar**: [Comprehensive guide title] (3000-4000 words)
- **Spoke 1**: [Specific sub-topic]
- **Spoke 2**: [Specific sub-topic]
- **Spoke 3**: [Specific sub-topic]
- **Spoke 4**: [Specific sub-topic]
- **Target keywords**: [primary keyword], [related keywords]
```

Each cluster should:
- Map to one of your audience segments
- Have a clear primary keyword with search volume
- Connect to your differentiation (what makes YOUR take unique)

## Step 4: Differentiation Strategy

Choose 1-2 primary differentiators:

| Differentiator | What it means | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Original data | You have data nobody else has | Internal metrics, surveys, experiments |
| Build-in-public | You show your actual work | Source code, decisions, failures |
| Practitioner depth | You've actually done the thing | Case studies from your work |
| Contrarian position | You disagree with consensus | Backed by evidence, not clickbait |
| First-principles | You derive from fundamentals | Mathematical proofs, traced code paths |
| Unique access | You interview/know the people | Expert quotes, insider perspective |

## Step 5: Content Scoring Targets

All published content must score 80+ on the quality rubric (see [scoring/quality-rubric.md](../scoring/quality-rubric.md)).

Posts scoring below 80 should be:
- Improved before publishing, OR
- Held as drafts until they can be improved, OR
- Killed (not everything deserves to be published)

## Step 6: Distribution Strategy

Content creation is 50% of the work. Distribution is the other 50%.

| Channel | Investment | Expected Return |
|---|---|---|
| Owned (blog, newsletter) | 40% | Long-term SEO, subscriber base |
| Earned (social, Reddit, communities) | 50% | Discovery, backlinks, AI citations |
| Paid (if applicable) | 10% | Amplification of best-performing content |

### Off-Site Presence (for AI Citation)

- YouTube companion content (0.737 correlation with AI citations)
- Reddit participation in relevant subreddits (450% growth in AI citation from Reddit)
- Review platform profiles (if B2B)
- Conference talks / podcast appearances (backlinks + authority)

## Step 7: Measurement

Track three layers:

### Traditional SEO Metrics
- Organic traffic (month over month)
- Keyword rankings (target top 5 for cluster keywords)
- Backlinks acquired
- Domain authority trend

### AI Citation Metrics
- Mentions in ChatGPT responses (search your brand/topic)
- Perplexity citations
- Google AI Overview inclusions
- Content freshness (% of posts updated within 30 days)

### Business Metrics
- Newsletter subscribers from blog
- Leads / conversions attributed to content
- Community engagement (comments, shares, discussions)
- Time to publish (efficiency)

## Step 8: 90-Day Roadmap

```markdown
### Month 1: Foundation
- Publish pillar article for Cluster 1
- Publish 2 supporting articles
- Set up distribution channels (LinkedIn profile, Medium account, newsletter)
- Baseline metrics

### Month 2: Momentum
- Publish pillar article for Cluster 2
- Publish 3 supporting articles
- Begin regular social distribution cadence
- First content refresh (update pillar 1 with new data)

### Month 3: Optimization
- Publish 3-4 supporting articles across clusters
- Analyze what's working (traffic, engagement, citations)
- Double down on winning topics/formats
- Plan Month 4-6 based on data
```

## Guardrails

1. Strategy without execution is worthless. Keep the plan achievable.
2. Don't spread across too many clusters. Depth beats breadth for authority.
3. Revisit strategy quarterly. Kill clusters that aren't performing.
4. Never sacrifice quality for quantity. One great post beats five mediocre ones.
