# Pass 4: Style and Voice

Surface polish, tone consistency, and AI-pattern detection.

## Checklist

- [ ] **Tone consistent**: No inappropriate shifts between sections
- [ ] **Voice appropriate**: Matches audience and genre expectations
- [ ] **Sentence variety**: Mix of lengths and structures (not monotonous)
- [ ] **Active voice predominates**: Passive only where appropriate
- [ ] **No clutter**: Redundancies, filler words, and deadwood removed
- [ ] **No AI patterns**: Text does not read as machine-generated

## AI-Pattern Detection

Flag any of these common AI writing signatures:

- Sycophantic openers ("Great question!", "Absolutely!")
- Formulaic structure (identical paragraph shapes throughout)
- Hedge stacking ("It's important to note that it might be worth considering...")
- Empty transitions ("Furthermore," "Moreover," "Additionally," used as filler)
- Hyperbolic claims about importance ("This is crucial/vital/essential")
- Bullet-point-itis (lists where prose would be more natural)
- Summary paragraphs that add nothing ("In conclusion, we have seen that...")
- Overly balanced "on the other hand" structures without commitment
- Emoji or exclamation marks where tone doesn't warrant them

For the comprehensive AI-pattern reference, see the humanized-writing skill's `foundations/signs-of-ai-writing.md`.

## Proofreading

- Spelling errors
- Grammar issues (subject-verb agreement, tense consistency, pronoun clarity)
- Punctuation problems
- Formatting consistency (headings, lists, emphasis)
- Terminology consistency (same concept = same word throughout)
- Abbreviations defined before use

## Read-Aloud Test

Simulate reading the piece aloud. Flag:
- Sentences that trip over themselves
- Awkward rhythm or cadence
- Sections that lose momentum
- Phrases that sound unnatural when spoken
