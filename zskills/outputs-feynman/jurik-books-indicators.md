# Mark Jurik's Books: Relationship to His Trading Indicators

## Executive Summary

Mark Jurik produced at least four publications: three sold through Jurik Research (two books and one audio seminar) plus a separate book published by Irwin Professional Publishing in 1997 [1, 2, 3]. **None of these publications are primarily about his proprietary trading indicators** (JMA, VEL, CFB, RSX, DMX, WAV, DDR) [1]. Instead, they cover broader topics — general computerized trading methodology, neural network forecasting, and mathematical approaches to financial data analysis [2]. However, the books are deeply related to the *intellectual foundations* and *design philosophy* behind those indicators, even though they don't document the proprietary algorithms themselves.

The key distinction: the books teach the **principles** (signal processing, noise reduction, lag minimization, data preprocessing, phase analysis) that Jurik later commercialized as proprietary indicator products [2]. They are "how to think about indicators" books, not "how to use JMA/RSX/etc." manuals.

## The Publications

### 1. *Advanced Computerized Trading Techniques: Using the Power of Technology to Manage Market Uncertainty and Maximize Profits* (1997)

- **Publisher:** Irwin Professional Pub. (ISBN prefix 0-7863) [3]
- **ISBN:** 9780786311712 [3]
- **Pages:** 320 [3, 8]
- **Format:** Hardcover [3]
- **Published:** 1997 (Goodreads says June 1997 [8]; Open Library says April 1997 [3])
- **OCLC:** 230954642 [3]
- **Provenance note:** The Open Library record for this edition was imported from Amazon product data, not from a library catalog record [3]. The Goodreads entry may derive from the same Amazon listing.

This appears to be a **distinct, earlier book** — separate from the 1999 *Computerized Trading* volume. The evidence: different publisher (Irwin vs. New York Institute of Finance), different ISBN prefix, different page count (320 vs. 415), and a substantively different title [3, 4, 8]. The Goodreads description states it "demonstrates how to backtest a system once it is in place; discusses trading in the real world; and explains how to understnad [sic] and use complex indicators" [8].

Notably, this book **does not appear on the jurikres.com catalog** [1, 2], which lists only two books and one audio seminar. This may indicate it was published through a mainstream publisher and is not directly part of the Jurik Research product line.

**Relationship to Jurik indicators:** The Goodreads description mentions "complex indicators" and backtesting, which aligns with the conceptual domain of Jurik's products, but no source connects this book to his specific proprietary indicators (JMA, RSX, etc.). Without a full table of contents, this assessment remains uncertain.

**Caveat:** This book has very limited online presence. Beyond the Open Library edition record and Goodreads entry, no independent reviews, library catalog descriptions, or bookseller listings with detailed contents were found. It is possible (though unlikely, given the different publisher and ISBN) that this is a cataloging artifact related to the 1999 book.

### 2. *Computerized Trading: Maximizing Day Trading and Overnight Profits* (1999)

- **Publisher:** New York Institute of Finance [4]
- **ISBN:** 9780735200777 [6]
- **Pages:** 415 [4]
- **Role:** Mark Jurik is the **editor**, not sole author [2]
- **Format:** Hardcover (now out of print / sold out) [2]
- **Status on jurikres.com:** "100% SOLD OUT — Currently not available" [2]

This is a **multi-author edited volume**. The jurikres.com description states "Mark Jurik taps into the minds of twenty experts" [2]. The table of contents lists 20 unique named contributors — 19 chapter authors plus Jurik himself, who contributed appendix entries on data sources and resources [2]. (The "twenty experts" phrasing may refer to 20 others besides Jurik, or may include him — the source is ambiguous.) The contributing authors include DiNapoli, Melancon, Vomund, Widner, Ang, Luisi, De La Maza, Stendahl, Kiev, Hayes, Kase, Antonacci, Strasser, May, Klimasauskas, Drake, Katz, Marder, and Alotta [2].

**Contents organized in four sections** [2]:
1. **Basic Trading Skills and Methods** — market entry/exit, popular indicators, trend analysis, money management
2. **Testing and Evaluation** — backtesting, evaluating trading performance
3. **Assessing the Market and Yourself** — trading psychology, market potential quantification, bar length selection
4. **Advanced Indicators and Forecasting** — intermarket analysis, nonlinear pricing, data preprocessing, statistical data mining, building advanced trading systems

**Relationship to Jurik indicators:** Indirect. The book discusses general indicator design principles and trading system development. Chapter topics like "Popular Trading Indicators" (by Melancon), "Complex Indicators" (by May), and "Data Preprocessing" (by Klimasauskas) cover the conceptual territory that Jurik's proprietary tools address — but they do not describe JMA, RSX, CFB, or any other Jurik-branded product [2]. Jurik's own contributions are limited to appendix material on data sources and resources [2].

### 3. *Neural Networks & Financial Forecasting* (report collection)

- **Publisher:** Jurik Research (self-published, not available in bookstores) [2]
- **Role:** Mark Jurik is the **author** of most reports in this collection [2]
- **Format:** Bound collection of published and unpublished technical reports [2]
- **Status on jurikres.com:** "100% SOLD OUT — Currently not available" [2]
- **Independent corroboration:** This publication is attested only by jurikres.com. No library catalog, review, or independent bookseller listing was found beyond the jurikres.com product page.

This is a **collection of Jurik's own research papers and technical reports**, described as covering "neural networks and fuzzy logic, as well as techniques and tricks on forecasting that Jurik Research reveals to its biggest clients" [2].

**Contents include 17 reports** [2]:
- "Wall Street Forecast with a Neural Network" — introduction to neural net financial applications
- "Consumer Guide to Software for Smart Forecasting" — reviews of modeling software
- "Indicator Development Issues in the Space Domain" — advanced techniques for financial data analysis (also published in *AI in Finance* and the book *Virtual Trading*) [2]
- Methodology Reports #1-3 on train/test performance, data partitioning, and scoring trading systems
- Application Notes #1-2 on optimal phase in crossover moving averages and efficient trading with adaptive moving averages
- "Estimating Optimal Forecast Distance using Chaos Analysis"
- Reports on neural networks, regression, backpropagation algorithm and its critique
- "Executive Overview of Neural Nets and Fuzzy Logic"
- "Technical Discussion of Neural Nets and Fuzzy Logic"
- "Backpercolation" — introducing an alternative neural net training algorithm
- "Backpercolation versus Backpropagation" — one-page summary

**Relationship to Jurik indicators:** This is the most directly relevant publication. Several reports address the exact problems that Jurik's commercial indicators solve:
- **Application Note #2** explicitly "compares the result with Jurik's moving average" [2] — a direct early reference to what became JMA.
- The **indicator development** report covers data preprocessing techniques relevant to WAV and DDR's domain.
- The **chaos analysis** report touches on optimal forecast horizons, conceptually related to CFB's fractal analysis approach.
- However, the reports describe *methodological principles*, not the proprietary algorithms themselves. The commercial products (JMA, RSX, etc.) are implementations of ideas discussed in these research papers, but the papers do not document the final commercial implementations.

### 4. *Space, Time, Cycles & Phase* (audio seminar, 1995)

- **Publisher:** Jurik Research [2]
- **Format:** MP3 audio file on CD, with printed copy of all visuals [2]
- **Origin:** Recording of a lecture at the 1995 Futures International Conference in California [2]
- **Status on jurikres.com:** Available from Jurik Research only [2]
- **Independent corroboration:** Like the report collection above, this publication is attested only by jurikres.com. No independent library catalog or review was found.

This is a **recorded seminar**, not a book in the traditional sense, though jurikres.com catalogs it alongside the books [1, 2]. It presents four mathematical frameworks for analyzing financial time-series data [2]. The material is based on Jurik's published article "Developing Indicators for Financial Trading" [2].

**Topics covered** [2]:
- **Space:** geometric perspective, the correct way to decorrelate data
- **Time:** optimal sampling, temporal compression
- **Cycles:** filtering, frequency spectrum, low-lag data smoothing
- **Phase:** phase-shifting the MACD, applications of phase diagrams

Also covers: limitations of standard regression, introduction to neural networks, flexibility vs. reliability, and a "master flow chart for model building" [2].

**Relationship to Jurik indicators:** Strongly foundational. The lecture explicitly covers **low-lag data smoothing** (the core JMA concept) and **filtering and the frequency spectrum** (relevant to RSX and VEL) [2]. The seminar's "data decorrelation" topic is also topically related to DDR (Data Decorrelation and Reduction), though the seminar predates DDR's release and does not mention it by name — this connection is an inference based on shared subject matter. This is perhaps the closest any Jurik publication comes to explaining *why* his indicators work the way they do.

## Additional Publications (Not Books)

Beyond the four publications above, Jurik also produced:
- **Free technical reports** (PDFs) on jurikres.com, including "The BIG Picture," "Why Use JMA?", "Evolution of Moving Averages," "Optimal Forecast Horizon," and others [5]
- **Articles** in *Futures* magazine and *Neurovest* journal (per Jurik's self-authored bio on jurikres.com [2]; "Neurovest journal" could not be independently verified as a publication name)
- **A chapter** in the book *Virtual Trading* (the "Indicator Development Issues in the Space Domain" report) [2]
- **Tutorial strategies** — 13 demonstration trading strategies with TradeStation/MultiCharts code showing how to use Jurik Tools [1]

The free technical reports are the publications most directly about his proprietary indicators. "Why Use JMA?" and "Evolution of Moving Averages" explicitly benchmark JMA against other filter designs [5]. These are marketing/educational documents rather than books, but they are the closest thing to indicator-specific publications Jurik produced.

**Bottom line:** Jurik's books are about the *science of indicator design and trading system development*, not about his specific commercial products. They teach the mathematical and conceptual foundations that his indicators are built on. The only publications that directly discuss JMA, RSX, and the other branded tools are the free PDF technical reports available on jurikres.com [5] — which we covered in our previous session's research.
